ERCOT POST-HOLIDAY DEMAND RAMP & TEXAS HEAT ONSET
⚡ ELECTRICITY · ERCOT

Industrial and commercial load returns across ERCOT Monday after the Memorial Day weekend as a developing heat ridge begins moving into the Dallas–Fort Worth corridor. Dallas highs are forecast to reach 96–100°F by Wednesday June 4, approximately 8–10°F above seasonal normal, pushing system demand from the holiday trough (~55–60 GW) toward the 70–75 GW range by midweek — territory that stress-tests ERCOT's 10.8% reserve margin. The 4CP (four coincident peak) tracking window effectively opens for transmission customers, meaning large industrial users will be actively shedding load during peak hours to avoid transmission cost allocations.

Watch for: ERCOT hourly load forecast updates Monday morning; any Emergency Condition or Conservation Watch notices; real-time price spikes above $150/MWh at North and Houston hubs during the 4–8 PM ramp. Battery storage dispatch capacity (~3.5 GW deployed) will be tested. If midday solar exceeds 24 GW and batteries charge fully by 3 PM, the evening ramp could be more severe than ERCOT's base forecast.

ERCOT SARA May 2026; NOAA CPC Heat Outlook; DTN Energy Weather; ERCOT.com Real-Time Dashboard

LNG FEEDGAS NOMINATIONS & GULF COAST INFRASTRUCTURE — HURRICANE SEASON OPENS
🌀 LNG · NATURAL GAS · WEATHER RISK

Two intersecting catalysts make LNG feedgas flows the most critical real-time data point heading into next week. First, Plaquemines LNG Phase 1 (Venture Global) is in active commercial ramp — Monday nominations will indicate whether the terminal has crossed the ~1.0 Bcf/d sustained throughput threshold, a meaningful upside catalyst for Henry Hub given the bearish storage backdrop. If the full LNG complex holds feedgas demand above 14.5 Bcf/d, it provides a tangible floor under prices even as storage injections remain robust.

Gulf of Mexico energy infrastructure (~1.8 MMbbl/d crude, 3.5 Bcf/d gas, Sabine Pass, Freeport, Corpus Christi, and Plaquemines LNG) begins elevated storm exposure immediately.
